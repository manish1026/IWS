What is IELTS?

IELTS stands for “International English Language Testing System” which is an English language test, globally conducted for non-native students and migrants to English-speaking nations. It is a proficiency test developed and run by the British Council in partnership with IDP Education and Cambridge Assessment English.

Why IELTS?

This test is follows an assessment criteria of English language that has helped millions of international students and global migrants worldwide to fulfill their dream to settle in developed English-speaking countries and achieve their career growth.

Which IELTS test I should take?
A. Academic IELTS

This test assesses formal English level that is taken by international students and working professionals who wish to work overseas.

B. General IELTS

General IELTS is generally taken by immigrants who wish to settle abroad. This is an assessment of English language usage in day-to-day life.

C. UKVI IELTS

IELTS for UKVI is intended for the aspirations who wish to study or settle specifically in the UK. Note: The test I should take and the band score requirements depend on the the country I wish to move, the visa requirements or the college/University/workplace to wish to study/ work with.

D. IELTS life skills (A1, A2 or B1)

Some immigrants such as visitors for any purposes like sportspersons, international business representatives, case of family extension( spouse, fiance, fiancee or dependents) need to take for IELTS life skills either for temporary or permanent settlement.
